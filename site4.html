SUBJECT: UNRESOLVED ANOMALIES — SECTOR B
1. Architectural Discrepancies

A routine survey reveals that physical corridor lengths do not match the official blueprints filed in city archives. Transit times between subterranean rooms are 40% longer than mapped. Tech teams report a "spatial expansion effect."

  • Corridor B-7 measures 34m on-site vs. 24m on record.
  • Sub-level 1 north wing: no mapped equivalent found.
  • Stairwell access to [REDACTED SUBLEVEL] has been sealed — origin of seal unknown.
NOTE: A civilian (Carla Ovidi) went missing in this sector on Tuesday. Camera loop 3B went dark exactly during the layout shift. Duration of blackout: unknown. Time of recovery: unknown.
2. Room 24 Specifications

The access door to Room 24 remains mechanically sealed. Despite zero power routing to this wing, the surrounding floor tiles exhibit persistent micro-vibrations and a constant temperature drop (–4°C).

  • No personnel have entered Room 24 since ██/██/19██.
  • Security patrols report a rhythmic metallic clicking from the interior.
  • True contents of the room remain unverified by current staff.
⚠ ALERT: Two (2) maintenance workers assigned to inspect the door perimeter have reported disorientation and partial memory loss following shifts in Sector B. Both have been placed on administrative leave pending psychological evaluation.
3. Reactionary Frequencies — "The Echo"

Acoustic monitoring has logged repetitive audio reflections in the ventilation ducts (resembling dragging sounds and low murmurs). These are officially classified as trapped residual noise.

However, field reports note these sounds actively alter their pitch and direction when a flashlight or radio frequency enters the corridor — suggesting a form of localized reaction.

⚠ TECH ADVISORY: VHS tapes recorded on-site create severe visual distortion on local CRT monitors. Playback authorized ONLY via isolated terminal screens. Do not freeze frames.
